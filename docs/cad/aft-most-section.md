# Aft-Most Cylindrical Section CAD Note

**Status:** First CAD export and joint-design intent captured.
**CAD source:** `hardware/cad/aft-most.step`
**Export source:** Onshape, `Aft-Most`, exported 2026-06-16.
**Units:** millimetres.

This note records the design intent behind the aft-most cylindrical section so that the CAD can evolve without losing the reasoning behind the current hole layout, stringer strategy, and heat-set insert stack-up.

---

## 1. Role in the Airframe

This part is the aft-most cylindrical rocket section. It is the first cylindrical section when building from the aft end upward. A cosmetic-style engine mount or motor-mount-like assembly is expected to attach behind it later, but this part is currently treated as an airframe section joint and structural interface.

The section is planned for PLA+ printing.

## 3. Section-to-Section Joint Intent

The section joint separates two structural jobs:

1. The 8 bolts provide the axial clamping force between printed sections.
2. The 4 aluminium stringers provide bending stiffness and alignment support between sections.

The stringers should not be the primary clamping mechanism and should not axially overconstrain the joint.

Current intended stringer stack:

```text
lower section stringer engagement: 100 mm
upper section stringer engagement: 100 mm
free axial clearance above stringer: 1 mm
total stringer length: 200 mm
upper section socket depth: 101 mm
```

This leaves the 8-bolt ring responsible for clamp-up while allowing the stringers to support bending without being hard-trapped as axial clamps.

---

## 4. Heat-Set Insert Joint Concept

The default printed-section joint uses a thin flange and a thick flange:

```text
screw head / washer side
        |
        v
+-----------------------+
| thin flange           |  screw clearance hole
+-----------------------+
| thick flange / boss   |  heat-set insert pilot hole
| heat-set insert       |  brass threaded insert
+-----------------------+
```

The thin flange is the pass-through side. The thick flange provides the insert support and the threaded reaction surface. The screw should pass freely through the thin flange and thread into the brass insert in the thick flange.

The screw must not bottom out inside the insert. Clamp load should be generated by the screw head or washer bearing on the clamped part while the threads pull against the insert. Bottoming out would turn torque into axial jacking of the insert/plastic instead of clamp load.

## 5. Parameters To Lock

The exact CAD dimensions for the joint should be locked only after selecting:

| Parameter | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `D_screw` | Nominal screw diameter, e.g. 3 mm for M3 |
| `L_screw` | Screw length under head |
| `head_style` | Socket head, button head, countersunk, etc. |
| `washer_OD` | Washer outer diameter, if used |
| `L_insert` | Heat-set insert length |
| `D_insert_pilot` | Manufacturer-recommended pilot hole diameter |
| `D_insert_OD_max` | Maximum outer knurl diameter of insert |
| `insert_install_side` | Whether insert is installed from the thick-flange side or through the thin-flange side |
| `T_clamped_external` | Thickness of any external mating part under the screw head |
| `T_thin` | Thin flange thickness |
| `T_thick` | Thick flange / insert boss depth |

The insert pilot diameter should come from the selected insert's datasheet or vendor drawing, not from the nominal screw diameter alone.

---

## 6. Driven CAD Formulas

### 6.1 Thin Flange Hole

If the insert is installed from the thick-flange side:

```text
thin_flange_hole_diameter = normal_clearance(D_screw)
```

Typical starting points:

```text
M3 clearance: 3.2 to 3.5 mm
M4 clearance: 4.3 to 4.7 mm
```

If the insert must be installed through the thin-flange side, the thin flange also needs an insert-access relief:

```text
thin_flange_relief_diameter >= D_insert_OD_max + 0.2 to 0.5 mm
```

That relief is not the same design feature as the screw clearance hole.

### 6.2 Thick Flange Insert Pilot

```text
thick_flange_pilot_diameter = D_insert_pilot
```

This value is insert-specific. For example, two different M3 heat-set inserts can require different pilot diameters because their knurl geometry and outer diameters differ.

### 6.3 Thick Flange Depth

Minimum boss depth for a blind heat-set insert:

```text
T_thick_min = L_insert + bottom_clearance + lead_in_allowance
```

Starting assumptions:

```text
bottom_clearance = 0.5 to 1.0 mm
lead_in_allowance = 0.3 to 0.8 mm
```

So:

```text
T_thick_min ~= L_insert + 1.0 to 2.0 mm
```

The current thick flange depth is approximately 15 mm and is arbitrary. It is likely generous for common M3 or M4 inserts, but final thickness should also consider plastic strength, boss outer diameter, mass, and assembly access.

### 6.4 Screw Length

The screw length should be selected after the insert and stack thickness are known:

```text
L_screw = T_clamped_external + T_thin + desired_thread_engagement
```

For a blind insert:

```text
desired_thread_engagement <= L_insert - 0.5 mm
```

Preferred screw-tip clearance:

```text
0.5 to 1.0 mm before the insert bottom
```

Do not use screw bottoming as depth control.

---

## 7. Rationale

- Thin flange holes should be screw clearance holes so the screw clamps the joint instead of threading into or dragging against printed plastic.
- Thick flange holes should be heat-set insert pilot holes because the brass insert provides the reusable threaded surface.
- Insert pilot dimensions must follow the chosen insert geometry.
- Thick flange depth must allow the insert to seat fully and leave room for displaced softened plastic.
- Screw length must provide thread engagement without bottoming out.
- The 8-bolt ring provides clamp load; the 4 aluminium stringers provide bending support and alignment without acting as axial clamps.
